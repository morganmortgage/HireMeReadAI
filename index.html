Why I’m excited about Read AI

Today’s mortgage journey is backwards. Buyers fall in love with a home, then scramble for a pre-approval, juggle quotes from a few lenders, and try to compare PDFs and fee worksheets at midnight. Behind the scenes, loan officers, realtors, and processors are bouncing between systems that don’t communicate: LOS, pricing engines, POS, CRM, portals, email, chat, spreadsheets.

I live inside that mess every day. Smart buyers overpay or miss opportunities not because they’re unqualified, but because the decision layer is broken. Nobody has a single, trusted brain that sits across the conversation and the software stack and says: “Here’s what’s really happening, here are the best structures for this borrower, and here’s what you should do next.”

Read AI is the closest thing I’ve seen to that missing brain. You already sit across meetings, email, and messages; you already summarize the noise and surface what matters. Plug that same copilot into the mortgage & real estate stack, and you don’t just save time – you change how home-financing decisions get made.

What I want to build at Read AI

  • A connected decision layer for home financing. Use Read’s position across meetings, email, and messages to build a single source of truth for each file: goals, constraints, pricing options, conditions, and trade-offs – one copilot view instead of scattered notes and systems.
  • Consumer-grade clarity on “which option is actually better?”. Turn the mess of loan estimates, fee worksheets, and lender jargon into side-by-side narratives a human can understand: what you pay, what you save, the risk, and why one structure is smarter for this borrower.
  • Real-time radar for lenders and realtors. Give teams a live view of which files are stuck, who hasn’t been followed up with, which buyers are at risk of drifting to another lender, and where concessions or structure changes can save a deal.
  • Vertical playbooks that match reality, not theory. Map how top producers actually work – including the hacks – and design copilot flows that remove manual note-taking, status updates, and repetitive explanations from their week.
  • A data flywheel that gets smarter with every file. Use patterns across thousands of loans and conversations to recommend better structures, spot hidden savings, and flag risk earlier – while keeping humans in control of the decision.
