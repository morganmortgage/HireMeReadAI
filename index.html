Why I’m excited about AI copilots in mortgage & real estate

Most buyers still choose their mortgage with limited data, rushed timelines, and little ability to compare outcomes. Servicers and originators sit on mountains of information, yet consumers navigate the largest financial decision of their lives with tools that feel like they’re from 2005.

I’ve spent my career inside this system — as a wholesale mortgage broker, branch manager, and educator. I know where the data lives, where it dies, and where borrowers get overcharged. Over the last 18 months, I’ve rebuilt my own practice around AI copilots that:

  • Turn meetings, emails, and docs into structured tasks, timelines, and conditions.
  • Compare complex scenarios in seconds — rate, term, MI, credits, and timing.
  • Explain options in plain language buyers, realtors, and CPAs actually understand.

My goal now is to partner with AI product teams that want to turn this inside-the-trenches knowledge into vertical copilots and decision engines for buyers, homeowners, and industry professionals.

What I want to build with an AI team

1. Copilots for real mortgage & real-estate workflows.

  • Buyer consult copilots that listen, summarize, flag risks, and auto-create conditions, tasks, and timelines.
  • Refinance & restructure copilots that watch rates, equity, and goals, then notify when “it finally makes sense.”
  • Agent & LO tools that stitch together meetings, email, CRM, LOS, and pricing engines into one coherent story.

2. Consumer-first decision tools.

  • Transparent wholesale vs. retail comparisons that expose lifetime cost, not just monthly payment.
  • Offer-structuring sandboxes: “If I raise price and ask for credits, what happens to cash-to-close and long-term cost?”
  • Smart alerts that blend property data, rate movement, and personal goals — not just “rates dropped, time to refi.”

3. Systems that pros actually adopt.

  • Tools that remove busywork instead of just creating new dashboards.
  • Workflows that fit how LOs, realtors, and ops teams already work — then quietly make everything faster and safer.
